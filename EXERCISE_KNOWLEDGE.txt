PORCHFITNESS EXERCISE GUIDE - AI Coach Knowledge Base

About PorchFitness:
Voice-coached chair exercises designed for seniors and anyone who wants accessible fitness. All exercises can be done seated or standing on a porch, patio, or at home.

Target Audience:
- Seniors and older adults
- People with limited mobility
- Anyone recovering from injury
- Beginners to fitness

15 EXERCISES (12 Seated + 3 Standing):

SEATED EXERCISES:

1. SEATED PIRIFORMIS STRETCH (MODIFIED)
- Benefits: Hip flexibility, sciatica relief, lower back tension
- Instructions: Sit upright, cross ankle over opposite knee (figure 4), gently press down on raised knee
- Duration: Hold 20-30 seconds each side
- Modifications: Don't force the knee down, keep back straight

2. SEATED HAMSTRING STRETCH
- Benefits: Leg flexibility, prevents stiffness, improves posture
- Instructions: Sit on edge of chair, extend one leg straight, gently reach toward toes
- Duration: Hold 20-30 seconds each side
- Modifications: Keep knee slightly bent if tight, never bounce

3. SIT TO STANDS
- Benefits: Leg strength, balance, functional movement
- Instructions: Sit in chair, stand up without using hands, sit back down slowly
- Reps: 2 sets of 10 reps (20 total)
- Modifications: Use hands for support if needed, start with fewer reps

4. SEATED CAT-COW STRETCH
- Benefits: Spine mobility, back pain relief, breathing
- Instructions: Hands on knees, arch back (cow), round back (cat), breathe with movement
- Duration: 3 reps of 20 seconds (60 seconds total)
- Modifications: Keep movements gentle and pain-free

5. SEATED ARM RAISES
- Benefits: Shoulder strength, upper body mobility
- Instructions: Raise arms overhead slowly, lower with control
- Reps: 2 sets of 10 reps (20 total)
- Modifications: Hold light weights or water bottles for added challenge

6. SEATED ANKLE CIRCLES
- Benefits: Ankle mobility, circulation, reduces swelling
- Instructions: Lift foot off ground, rotate ankle in circles both directions
- Reps: 2 sets of 10 circles each direction, each ankle (20 total)
- Modifications: Point and flex if circles are difficult

7. SEATED SIDE BENDS
- Benefits: Core flexibility, spine lateral movement, breathing capacity
- Instructions: Reach one arm overhead, bend to opposite side, feel stretch in side body
- Duration: 3 reps of 20 seconds each side (60 seconds total)
- Modifications: Keep both hips seated, don't twist

8. SEATED SPINAL TWIST
- Benefits: Spine rotation, digestion, back health
- Instructions: Sit tall, twist torso to one side, hold chair back, look over shoulder
- Duration: 3 reps of 20 seconds each side (60 seconds total)
- Modifications: Keep movements gentle, don't force

9. FIGURE 4 STRETCH (DEEP HIP OPENER)
- Benefits: Deep hip stretch, glute flexibility, piriformis relief
- Instructions: Sit in chair, place ankle on opposite thigh, flex raised foot, lean forward from hips
- Duration: 3 reps of 20 seconds each side (60 seconds total)
- Modifications: Keep back straight, don't force the stretch

10. SEATED SHOULDER ROLLS
- Benefits: Shoulder tension relief, upper back mobility
- Instructions: Roll shoulders backward in large circles, then forward
- Reps: 2 sets of 10 rolls each direction (20 total)
- Modifications: Keep movements slow and controlled

11. SEATED NECK STRETCHES
- Benefits: Neck tension relief, range of motion
- Instructions: Gently tilt head to each side (ear toward shoulder), forward, and rotate side to side
- Duration: 3 reps of 20 seconds each direction (60 seconds total)
- Modifications: Never force, stop if dizzy, keep shoulders relaxed

12. SEATED MARCHING
- Benefits: Hip flexor strength, core engagement, circulation
- Instructions: Sit tall, lift one knee up toward chest, alternate legs in marching motion
- Reps: 2 sets of 10 reps each leg (20 total)
- Modifications: Lift knees only as high as comfortable, engage core

STANDING EXERCISES (with chair support):

13. STANDING HIP EXTENSION
- Benefits: Hip strength, glute activation, posture
- Instructions: Stand behind chair, hold for support, extend one leg straight back, squeeze glutes
- Reps: 2 sets of 10 reps each leg (20 total)
- Modifications: Keep movements controlled, don't arch back

14. STANDING SIDE BEND
- Benefits: Core flexibility, lateral spine movement, balance
- Instructions: Stand beside chair for support, reach one arm overhead, bend to opposite side
- Duration: 3 reps of 20 seconds each side (60 seconds total)
- Modifications: Hold chair with one hand, keep hips stable

15. STANDING CALF STRETCH
- Benefits: Ankle flexibility, calf health, Achilles tendon
- Instructions: Stand facing chair, place one foot back with heel down, gently lean forward
- Duration: 3 reps of 20 seconds each side (60 seconds total)
- Modifications: Reduce distance between feet, bend back knee slightly

SAFETY GUIDELINES:
- Always breathe - never hold breath
- Move slowly and with control
- Stop if you feel pain (mild discomfort is okay)
- Stay hydrated
- Consult doctor before starting new exercise program
- Use chair with armrests for extra support when needed

COACHING STYLE:
- Be encouraging and patient
- Use simple, clear language
- Check in frequently: "How does that feel?"
- Celebrate small wins
- Offer modifications freely
- Emphasize safety over intensity
- Make it conversational and friendly

COMMON QUESTIONS:
Q: Is this safe for seniors?
A: Yes! All exercises are low-impact and can be modified. Always consult your doctor first.

Q: I have limited mobility - can I still do these?
A: Absolutely! Most exercises are seated, and everything can be modified to your level.

Q: How often should I exercise?
A: Aim for 3-5 times per week, 15-30 minutes per session. Listen to your body.

Q: What if I feel pain?
A: Stop immediately. Mild discomfort is okay, but sharp pain means stop and rest.

Q: Can I do these if I have arthritis?
A: Many people with arthritis benefit from gentle movement. Start slow and consult your doctor.
